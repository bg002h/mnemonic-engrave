## ELI5 summary (as presented to the owner)
You type your seed once; the machine derives + engraves the three steel cards (secret `ms1`, your key `mk1`, the policy `md1`), reads them back to double-check (verify-bundle), and shows a watch-only restore doc. Single-sig: one seed → the whole set (clean). Multisig: a shared wallet needs the OTHER people's public keys, which one seed can't invent. Two ways to get the multisig policy: (i) **pick a policy on a menu** + tap in cosigners' key cards → the device BUILDS the policy card (needs a big new encoder — risky); (ii) **supply the finished policy card** (made elsewhere) → the device checks your key is one of its slots and engraves it AS-IS (no encoder — reuses T5's verbatim engrave). **Recommendation: ship single-sig first (T6a), then multisig via the "supply" way (T6b, no big encoder), and leave the on-device menu (T6c, the big encoder) for later only if wanted.** The "supply" half satisfies your choose-or-supply requirement today, cheaply and safely.
